The Tercer Informe de Gobierno is a report presented by the Government of Mexico to the Honorable Congress of the Union, providing an account of the state of the country's public administration. The report is based on the three general axes of the National Development Plan 2019-2024: Politics and Government, Social Policy, and Economy. \n\nIn the Politics and Government section, the report highlights actions taken to combat corruption, strengthen the rule of law, and separate public interest from economic power. In terms of security, the National Security Strategy aims to bring peace to citizens through coordinated efforts between different levels of government. The report shows a reduction in crime rates for several offenses. The government is committed to providing legal certainty for the participation of the Armed Forces in public security tasks, while respecting human rights and preserving national sovereignty.\n\nThe report also discusses efforts to strengthen democracy and citizen participation, such as the implementation of a popular consultation and ongoing dialogue with social groups. In terms of human rights, the government has taken steps to protect freedom of the press, freedom of expression, and religious beliefs. Progress has been made in promoting gender equality, combating gender-based violence, and eliminating discrimination against women and girls.\n\nIn the Social Policy section, the report covers various programs aimed at improving the well-being of vulnerable populations, including pension programs, support for children and working mothers, scholarships, and youth employment initiatives. It also addresses education and healthcare, with the establishment of the Institute of Health for Well-being and investments in cultural initiatives for peace and well-being.\n\nThe Economy section focuses on promoting economic growth, maintaining fiscal responsibility, improving tax collection, and encouraging private investment. It highlights the rescue of the energy sector, the promotion of economic recovery and job creation, and the creation of the Bank of Well-being. Other initiatives include the construction of rural roads, nationwide internet coverage, regional projects, the Santa Lucía International Airport, and the promotion of self-sufficiency in agriculture. The government also emphasizes the importance of science, technology, and sports as drivers of development.\n\nOverall, the report demonstrates the government's commitment to achieving inclusive and sustainable development in Mexico, with a focus on eradicating corruption, improving security, promoting social welfare, and fostering economic growth."
